Given an integer array nums, return _the number of**AND triples**_.

An **AND triple** is a triple of indices `(i, j, k)` such that:

  * `0 <= i < nums.length`
  * `0 <= j < nums.length`
  * `0 <= k < nums.length`
  * `nums[i] & nums[j] & nums[k] == 0`, where `&` represents the bitwise-AND operator.



**Example 1:**

> Input: nums = [2,1,3]
> 
> Output: 12
> 
> Explanation: We could choose the following i, j, k triples:
> 
> (i=0, j=0, k=1) : 2 & 2 & 1
> 
> (i=0, j=1, k=0) : 2 & 1 & 2
> 
> (i=0, j=1, k=1) : 2 & 1 & 1
> 
> (i=0, j=1, k=2) : 2 & 1 & 3
> 
> (i=0, j=2, k=1) : 2 & 3 & 1
> 
> (i=1, j=0, k=0) : 1 & 2 & 2
> 
> (i=1, j=0, k=1) : 1 & 2 & 1
> 
> (i=1, j=0, k=2) : 1 & 2 & 3
> 
> (i=1, j=1, k=0) : 1 & 1 & 2
> 
> (i=1, j=2, k=0) : 1 & 3 & 2
> 
> (i=2, j=0, k=1) : 3 & 2 & 1
> 
> (i=2, j=1, k=0) : 3 & 1 & 2

**Example 2:**

> Input: nums = [0,0,0]
> 
> Output: 27

**Constraints:**

  * `1 <= nums.length <= 1000`
  * `0 <= nums[i] < 216`
